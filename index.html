Conclusion

Australia’s elderly population has risen steadily since 2001, outpacing growth in public hospitals. State-level readiness varies markedly, with some states lagging behind national averages. The combined spatial, temporal, comparative and relational views show that healthcare capacity has not uniformly adapted to ageing.
