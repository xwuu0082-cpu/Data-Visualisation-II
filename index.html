Australia’s Ageing & Hospital Capacity

From 2001–2025, are public hospitals keeping pace with an ageing population across Australia?

Why this matters

Australia’s share of residents aged 65+ continues to rise, reshaping the demand for healthcare services. This page combines demographic and health infrastructure data to reveal where ageing is concentrated, how hospital capacity has changed, and whether availability matches need.

Where is ageing most concentrated?
Spatial overview by state

Older populations are concentrated in southern and coastal states such as Tasmania and South Australia. These areas likely face greater demand for hospital services as ageing accelerates.

Conclusion

Across space and time, ageing has outpaced hospital growth. While some states improved their capacity, others lag behind. The evidence suggests Australia must invest strategically in hospital infrastructure to ensure equitable care for an ageing nation.
